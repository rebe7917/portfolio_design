Fonts and Components

The assignment

In this assignment our focus was to find two fonts that worked well together – a display font and a paragraph font. I used google fonts to find the fonts. Then I used the website type-scale.com to find the right font hierarchy for the site. For the display font I used ‘Lora, serif’, and for the paragraph font I went with a sans-serif ‘Source Sans Pro’.

I chose a sans-serif for the paragraph as well as a line-height on 1.5. for better readability. For designing the layout on the cards, I used CSS flex, and then spaced the content evenly for a uniformed look on the cards. I used a small box-shadow on the cards to make them stand out from the background and rounded the borders to soften them up a bit.

Visuel details

Fonts

I chose a font with serifs and a thicker weight for the display font to make it stand out more. ‘Lora, serif’

For the paragraph I wanted a font that was very simple and clean. Therefor I chose a sans-serif font, where the letters weren’t particularly wide or had any extra details. I also added a line-height on 1.5 to make it more readable for users. ‘Source Sans Pro’

Buttons

For the CTA-buttons on the cards I chose to round the corners to give them a softer look, and break the otherwise square design on the site.

I gave the remove-button a soft red color, to make it more obvious that the action when clicked on is “negative”. Then added a light green background color to the accept-button to make it more obvious that the action when clicked on is “positive”
